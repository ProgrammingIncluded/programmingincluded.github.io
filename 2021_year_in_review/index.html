2021: The Story of His Glory

29th December 2021 at 8:17pm
2021 Year in Review Recap

Sing to the LORD, bless his name; tell of his salvation from day to day. Declare his glory among the nations, his marvelous works among all the peoples.

Psalm 96:2-3

I remember I was sitting on my desk, feeling the burden lifting as I came to realize this truth about two years ago during the Pandemic:

When salvation is man focused, the Gospel loses purpose and life changing power. Salvation is not primarily about saving people from hell.

I have just had the privilege going out of country to observe and serve my brothers in Kurdistan. If anything, I was deeply encouraged from them and it was more about them teaching me things than I ever had the privilege to learn. Coming back, I was both in culture shock but also most importantly, burdened. This burden, however I have come to realize, was interwoven with a duty and responsibility that stemmed from my own strength rather than from God. I hope that what I write below may resonate with folks and perhaps help them to find encouragement in the Scriptures.

Salvation is Focused on God

Salvation is first and foremost about God and His Glory. Why is this important and how is it lived out practically in the Christian? I will leave the many Scripture references to those who have come before me. Steven Hawthorne has done a great job expounding Scripture on this subject. Here are the notes from his article: (The Story of His Glory) You should also be able to find the articles online.

Scripture makes this truth known and apparent through out the various books. Ephesians 1:4b-10 is one such example.

In love 5 he predestined us for adoption to himself as sons through Jesus Christ, according to the purpose of his will, 6 to the praise of his glorious grace, with which he has blessed us in the Beloved. 7 In him we have redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of our trespasses, according to the riches of his grace, 8 which he lavished upon us, in all wisdom and insight 9 making known to us the mystery of his will, according to his purpose, which he set forth in Christ 10 as a plan for the fullness of time, to unite all things in him, things in heaven and things on earth.

Ephesians 1:4b-10

Man Powered Salvation

When salvation is rooted upon a desire first to see other people saved we find ourselves:

  • Potentially burned out because we fail to keep our center and attention on God.
  • Potentially depressed because not all will come to know Christ and many will be stubborn.
  • Potentially misguided in ministry philosophies focusing on the form rather than the function to better suit our culture or reasoning about numbers.

We need to be put Christ back to His proper name, a name that is above every other name (Philippians 2:9.)

God Powered Salvation

As Steve Hawthorne puts it:

People are indeed saved by the global declaration of God's salvation, but the ultimate value of their salvation is not to be seen in what they are saved from, it is what they are saved for that really matters.

Perspectives: The Story of His Glory pg 51

When one's heart is centered on God first and foremost, then we find ourselves focused on:

  • A desire to serve with proper hunger to learn more from Scriptures to know about who we serve.
  • A pursuit of holiness and the characteristics of the beatitudes that Christ outlines in Matthew 5:1-12
  • Ministries waging peace both eternally and temporally.
  • A proper care for and nurturing people with much love than we can possible accomplish by our strength. (See Cost of Discpleship Quote)

What I am not Advocating

What I am not advocating for is a call to only focus on inward outreach. Nor am I advocating for only looking outward. Those are the fruits of what is deeper from our heart: a focus on God and His glory. What we are to be saved "for" rather than saved "from".

This is not a call for people to stop evangelism to focus only on teaching. Nor a call for people to start evangelizing more. Both are appropriate and requires the Holy Spirit rather than our own strength. Both have a time and place. Plenty of Scriptures to backup both. The body has many gifts. What I am writing about is for people to pray and go through the Scriptures. To lift these things up to Christ and find burden lifting power to continue.

A Call to Action: God wants our Attention

As Piper once said: "Christians care about all suffering, especially eternal suffering." [1].

I end with a quote from Hawthorne which also nicely segues into the next section:

The rationale for mission seems simple enough: Since God is supreme, every creature should bow down in subjection. But can this really be the logic at the center of the universe? [...] The Scriptures are loud about the truth that God is love. God calls people to love Him with all that they are. Where is God's love, and ours, in response? A God who demands worship just because He's supreme doesn't seem like a very loving God. In fact, such a God might not seem like He is worthy even to be admired. God's penchant for praise might make Him appear to be struggling with a low self-image problem. It's foolish to speak of God's jealousy for worship as if He were a petulant tribal deity threatened by rival gods. God is not threatened; rather He is immeasurably saddened by false worship. When people worship anyone or anything besides Him, they become like it. God has better intentions for people.

Perspectives: The Story of His Glory pg 51 by Hawthorne.

What is Best for Us?

God desires us to turn to Him for worship. He wants our attention because it is what is best for us. When we turn our hearts to other things they seem pleasant at first, but we find ourselves trapped in a cage, lusting after those desires, needing another dose - another high to get away from the present distress.

We become the very things we desire after (Psalm 115:4-8), and at the end of that path is death. Observe only a child who has just found a new video game or a rock star and see their heart want to mimic the things they love. Likewise it is for us as humans, to long for the things we desire and want to "collect" or become like them. See also Romans 1:23 and 8:29. [3]

What would mankind become if we are to follow after someone who loves others even before we loved them (John 3:16)? What would mankind become when we are to follow someone named the "Prince of Peace?"

He first loved us so that we can love (1 John 4:19). The pursuit of God and subsequently holiness is of paramount importance if we are to follow Him.

The Idea of Risk in the World

At work I am often bombarded with the idea of "risk." If something is too "risky" then it isn't worth pursuing. But probe a bit further and it seems the "risk" is often tied to one's own success and failure. What makes one look good and not so good. Great effort is placed into marketing one's own efforts and trying to argue why things should go certain directions with blatant disregard to staffing, constraints, and other problems plaguing the team. Dishing out criticisms to another in an attempt to boost one's own position.

Many fail the litmus test of a "coach": a coach of a team wouldn't go and say, "I shall be the one to play all aspects of the sport and save the team." Coaches encourage and they lift up the team. A house divided cannot stand. Most decisions are made short-sighted. Rare it is to see character, integrity, and care for one another in the work place. However, those that I see who focus on integrity and genuineness find something more precious than gold or riches.

I write these things not to represent any specific institution, it happens throughout human history and almost every institution. This is human nature. See also the history of Disney for example [1] or the United Fruit Company [2].

The Irony of American History Observation

Niehr makes a strong quote for this observation in his analysis of American history. See following for full quote The Irony of American History Quote.

A Desire to be Loved

Many of the world today desires to be loved and feel loved (however they may be defined.) Yet many, when it comes to loving your neighbor as yourself, fail and/or find it exceedingly difficult often under their own standards.

Very Difficult to Love

I do think it is absolutely difficult, in keeping how humans work through history, to practice the beatitudes in a simplistic analysis by non-Christians. It takes extraordinary strength to stand against this tide and to become proclaimers of peace. To be meek before God and to be poor in Spirit. This is not something that comes naturally as history and contemporary times have shown. It is better for the natural man to just "get by" and follow one's happiness / desires.

The Politics Creeping In?

Our Christian habit is to bewail the world's deteriorating standards with an air of rather self-righteous dismay. 'The world is going down the drain,' we say with a shrug. But whose fault is it? Who is to blame? Let me put it like this. If the house is dark when nightfall comes, there is no sense in blaming the house, for that is what happens when the sun goes down. The question to ask is 'Where is the light?' Just so, if society deteriorates and standards decline till it becomes dark as night, there is no sense in blaming society, for that is what happens when fallen men and women are left to themselves and human selfishness is unchecked. The question to ask is 'Where is the Church? Why are the salt and light of Jesus Christ not permeating and changing society?

John Stott

We as Christians are humans (James 5:17) and cut from the same cloth so we are also to be greatly careful about how we think: the wisdom of man or the wisdom of God? It saddens me that this way of thinking not only pervades in the work place, but in the church which have often acted in unbelief. Tozer writes:

Once we surrender ourselves to God and allow His wisdom to be our wisdom, we have nothing more to fear. We can embrace the leadership and guidance of God the Holy Spirit as He leads us in a path that we probably could never imagine ourselves. I learned this early in my Christian life and ministry. When I was being interviewed for ordination, there were men on the committee who did not think I was qualified. They were right in many regards. I never finished high school; I went to one day of eight grade. I never went to college or seminary or any other thing of that nature. I just began preaching on the street corners as I believed God was leading me. I preached in summer tent meetings and on street corners and did all this to the glory of God. [...] After they had all talked about it, one man apparently said, "I don't know- I have a feeling about this young man. I think he has a real heart for God and for ministry. I think we should consider ordaining him and just put him in God's hands." [...] If we let God lead us and give up our own wisdom, He will lead us on a path that we never imagined we would be on.

"A Cloud By Day, A Fire By Night" Pg 66 by A. W. Tozer.

This is just one example. The church has always been under the problem of politics and human motivated wisdom since the days of early Catholic church even the days of Paul (1 Corinthians 3:4).

These arguments can become so loud and big that we neglect the other counsel of God. While these discussion happen, the voiceless continue to have no voice (see principles of fasting in Isaiah 58; good Samaritan parable, Proverbs 31:8-9 ) and the Gospel no longer is front and center.

What I am not Advocating For

Again, I need to put a warning, this is not me advocating for a certain fruit or action to manifest in your life today (though if the Spirit puts it on your heart, pray about it!). It does not mean to discount all wisdom given to you by counsel or by those around you. Those decisions requires earnest prayer, faith, and obedience that Tozer outlines as part of how God worked in his life.

There are many problems in the church and I believe it is no coincidence that of the churches in Revelation, only two were commended and those two churches were under persecution. But this is not a call to complain or list out every fault of the church as a proclamation. That we have many. We are, after all, in a spiritual hospital. Rather, how do we come before God knowing these truths and continue to find strength to walk with Him? Ultimately we need to come before Him.

The Call to Action

How do we navigate in this world when it seems our nature seems at odds against us? How do we be set apart from the world and practice righteousness?

Once more, I think we have lost sight of who we are saved "for" and instead have focused on what to be saved "from." Tozer writes with sharp clarity:

The doctrine of justification by faith- a biblical truth, and a blessed relief from sterile legalism and unavailing self-effort- has in our time fallen into evil company and been interpreted by many in such manner as actually to bar men from the knowledge of God. The whole transaction of religious conversion has been made mechanical and spiritless. Faith may now be exercised without a jar to the moral life and without embarrassment to the Adamic ego. Christ may be "received" without creating any special love for Him in the soul of the receiver. The man is "saved," but he is not hungry nor thirsty after God. In fact he is specifically taught to be satisfied and encouraged to be content with little. The modern scientist has lost God amid the wonders of His world; we Christians are in real danger of losing God amid the wonders of His Word. We have almost forgotten that God is a Person and, as such, can be cultivated as any person can. It is inherent in personality to be able to know other personalities, but full knowledge of one personality by another cannot be achieved in one encounter. It is only after long and loving mental intercourse that the full possibilities of both can be explored.

The Pursuit of God pg 22-23 by A. W. Tozer

How is your relationship with the Lord? How is your private prayer closet? Can you confidently be like the Psalmist and proclaim:

121 I have done what is just and right; do not leave me to my oppressors. 122 Give your servant a pledge of good; let not the insolent oppress me. 123 My eyes long for your salvation and for the fulfillment of your righteous promise. 124 Deal with your servant according to your steadfast love, and teach me your statutes. 125 I am your servant; give me understanding, that I may know your testimonies!

Psalm 119:121-125

I still have much to learn on this but am convicted to write and spur others to continue as others have come before.

The Knowledge of the Assurance of Salvation?

Are you still stuck trying to figure out if you truly saved? There could be many reasons for this and I cannot guess what is going on in your life. However I would like to share one realization I had to really help encourage me to continue walking in the faith.

Many quote Philippians 2:12 and stop. It can be almost a bit worry some as our imaginations takes hold and end up perhaps causing the Christian to focus on the first half. To not put into action the life giving words of Christ. That is not the full picture, for there is a second half due to the "for":

12 Therefore, my beloved, as you have always obeyed, so now, not only as in my presence but much more in my absence, work out your own salvation with fear and trembling, 13 for it is God who works in you, both to will and to work for his good pleasure.

Philippians 2:12-13

Both are important. The Lord we serve is powerful and someone to be feared so we ought to work out our salvation with "fear and trembling" BECAUSE ("for") God, not any one else, works in us "both to will and to work for his good pleasure." We do not work our way into the Kingdom. In the great inspired writing, Paul both acknowledges the divine intervention of God and our part for obedience.

It is in this verse we realize more than just one heart condition when a Christian questions his faith, it is perhaps a failure to recognize the promises of God or we have neglected His truth.

Could it be that we were never Christ's as the many ask and use this verse to urge us to examine ourselves? Perhaps, but just like the question, "how do we love our wife/husbands?" that question will be ever present. But this passage focuses on what is being saved "for" the goal that we should press forward not to be saved "from." God is to be found faithful as He first loved us. See [9] for the exposition here.

The Pursuit of God and Holiness

In the prior section we had discussed the importance of the pursuit of God. Why is this important? How does the pursuit of God tie back into the pursuit of Holiness or to practice what is righteous?

James on the topic of fighting and disagreements he writes:

Therefore it says, “God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble.” 7 Submit yourselves therefore to God. Resist the devil, and he will flee from you. 8 Draw near to God, and he will draw near to you. Cleanse your hands, you sinners, and purify your hearts, you double-minded. 9 Be wretched and mourn and weep. Let your laughter be turned to mourning and your joy to gloom. 10 Humble yourselves before the Lord, and he will exalt you.

James 4:6b-10

When we argue and things don't go our way, perhaps we let the sun go down on our anger because we have failed to relinquish our control to God. We should acknowledge Him in all our ways. To come humbly before Him and let Him be our protector.

We find ourselves in the very position of Christ's teachings:

3 Blessed are the poor in spirit, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven. 4 Blessed are those who mourn, for they will be comforted. 5 Blessed are the meek, for they will inherit the earth. 6 Blessed are those who hunger and thirst after righteousness, for they will be filled.

Matthew 5:3-6

When our hearts are turned to God and desire to worship before Him first, we find ourselves longing for the things after which He cares about.

I would like to point out the fourth beatitude: "Blessed are those who hunger and thirst after righteousness, for they will be filled." There-in lies the importance of holiness. In order to understand what is righteous and to hunger and thirst for it, it requires a realistic view of life and practice:

But solid food is for the mature, for those who have their powers of discernment trained by constant practice to distinguish good from evil.

Hebrews 5:14

How can a Christian expect to pursue holiness if we have not yet pursued God? Furthermore, how can a Christian long for righteousness if discernment has not yet been continuously put into practice by what we do?

The pursuit of God is different for different people. A Samson is different from a Joseph is different from Abraham. But read into their lives and there is undeniable proof of their faith put into obedience. Such faithful obedience could be a blessing to thousands. So is my prayer for my fellow brothers and sisters.

God wanted Abram's utmost devotion and attention, to the point of even purifying the love for his one and only son, Isaac. For Abram, he rationalized this as God being able to bring Isaac to life (and indeed He could!) and thus allowed Abram to be obedient to God's directive. But we know now that it was a test and thus Abram's only love of his son was seen in light of his devotion to God and thus his name became Abraham (see the Pursuit of God by Tozer about Abraham.)

In the past two sections I have focused on how the Christian is really motivated by the pursuit of God in all things so as to find their source of strength. Salvation "for" instead of salvation "from."

Once again, my heart goes out to those going through suffering. I write this not because I claim to know the difficulties you are going through or have gone through. With suffering the emotions are real and I cannot discount them.

Many others have written this topic far better than I have so I would only like to leave a quote. I hope in writing this, those who have already gone through or am out of the storm can perhaps interpret them in new light. If you are still going through things, thank you for reading this far but please do take the time to rest and recharge.

I am writing because I wish to shed some light on suffering that we as Christians can understand and can potentially point us to more strength.

The hammer is a useful tool, but the nail, if it had feeling and intelligence could present another side of the story. For the nail knows the hammer only as an opponent, a brutal, merciless enemy who lives to pound it into submission, to beat it down out of sight and clinch it into place. That is the nail's view of the hammer, and it is accurate except for one thing: The nail forgets that both it and the hammer are servants of the same workman. Let the nail but remember that the hammer is held by the workman and all resentment toward it will disappear. The carpenter decides whose head shall be beaten next and what hammer shall be used in the beating. That is his soverieng right. When the nail has surrendered to the will of the workman and has gotten a little glimpse of his benign plans for its future, it will yield to the hammer without complaint.

The file is more painful still, for its business is to bite into the soft metal, scraping and eating away the edges till it has shaped the metal to its will. Yet the file has, in truth, no real will in the matter, but serves another master as the metal also does. It is the master and not the file that decides how much shall be eaten away, what shape the metal shall take and how long the painful filing shall continue. Let the metal accept the will of the master and it will try not to dictate when or how it shall be filed. [...]

With all this known to him, how could Rutherford find it in his heart to praise God for the hamer, the file and the furnace? The answer is simply that he loved the Master of the hammer, he adored the Workman who wielded the file, he worshiped the Lord who heated the furnace for the everlasting blessing of His children. He had felt the hammer till its rough beatings no longer hurt; he had endured the file till he had come actually to enjoy its bitings; he had walked with God in the furnace so long that it has become as his natural habitat.

"The Root of Righteousness" pg 163 A. W. Tozer

Many of the sufferings we go through in this life, as a Christian, I can view as purposeful and trust in the Lord through this all as I gaze upon Him. This is the power of salvation "for" instead of salvation "from." God exalting and God centered salvation brings upon power for our souls to continue.

A Bit More on One's Own Knowledge of the Assurance of Salvation

The question of one's own knowledge of the assurance of salvation is something left unsatisfactory for a person who seeks desiring a solution that is scientific and almost formulaic. [Note: Books like 1 John and other passages focuses on our fruit or our confession of Christ (which is a fruit, an action on our part for obedience). For many this is satisfactory. But for myself and other thinkers, we think also about passages like Matthew 7 :21-23 about the dangers of deceiving ourselves. Subscribing to a formulaic process, e.g. if I do XYZ then I have salvation does not exist because our salvation does not come from ourselves. This is what I mean by 100% know our own salvation.]

We cannot 100% know our own salvation unless God reveals it to us as the author and perfector of our Faith either miraculous or not. He has at times throughout history within Scriptures done so prophetically (I am thinking of Samuel and others. Though the exact texts / reasoning I have not cited for this section so please let me know if otherwise.)

As a proclaiming follower of Christ who seeks to be obedient, it seems to me unwise even unhealthy to dwell on one's own salvation rather than to trust in God's work in us once we have confessed Him to be our Lord. Again, is our salvation focusing on the "from" or the "for?" Paul is asking us to address our obedience not our state of salvation. "Work out" not infer.

Our Relationship with God and the Fruits: Why Infering Just Doesn't Work

Allow me for a bit to explain why this rabbit hole could be potentially so dangerous.

Isn't what our relationship with the Lord, our condition before Him similar to that of a relationship? How do we love our family? Our friends? How do we know "know"? Is it by words alone?

No. It is by the actions and by time. A perusal of different verses in Scripture and we see that very identity: the focus on the fruits of one's life (see a list in [1]) or the perseverance of the saints through trials (Hebrews 3:6). It also doesn't help that we stumble and fall (1 John 2:4).

We are in much better hands of salvation before God who is both Love and Righteousness than we are before ourselves. He desires for all to be saved (1 Timothy 2:3-4; Ezekiel 18:23) and He has promised those who accept Him will be saved (Romans 10:9). (See also [2] Piper's book goes into this more in-depth by looking at a wider range of Scriptures and talk about God's different wills in respect to those passages and election.)

It is on those promises that I continue forward walking with Him.

The Irony of American History Quote

29th December 2021 at 9:33pm
Reinhold Niebuhr

Practically all schools of modern culture, whatever their differences, are united in their rejection of the Christian doctrine of original sin. This doctrine asserts the obvious fact that all men are persistently inclined to regard themselves more highly and are more assiduously concerned with their own interests than any 'objective' view on their importance would warrant. Modern culture in its various forms feels certain that, if men could be sufficiently objective or disinterested to recognize the injustice of excessive self-interest, they could also in time transfer the objectivity of their judgments as observers of the human scene to their judgements as actors and agents in human history. This is an absurd notion which every practical statesman or man of affairs knows how to discount because he encounters ambitions and passions in his daily experience, which refute the regnant modern theory of potentially innocent men and nations.

There is consequently a remarkable hiatus between the shrewdness of practical men of affairs and the speculations of our wise men. The latter are frequently convinced that the predicament of our possible involvement in an atomic and global conflict is due primarily to failure of the statesmen to heed the advice of our psychological and social scientists. The statesmen on the other hand have fortunately been able to disregard the admonition of our wise men because they could still draw upon the native shrewdness of the common people who in smaller realms have had something of the same experience with human nature as the statesmen. The statesmen have not been particularly brilliant in finding solutions for our problems, all of which have reached global dimensions. But they have, at least, steered a course which still offers us minimal hope of avoiding a global conflict. But whether or not we avoid another war, we are covered with prospective guilt.

We have dreamed of a purely rational adjustment of interests in human society; and we are involved in 'total' wars. We have dreamed of a 'scientific' approach to all human problems; and we find that the tensions of a world-wide conflict release individual and collective emotions not easily brought under reactional control. We had hoped to make neat and sharp distinctions between justice and injustice; and we discover that even the best human actions involve some guilt.

Reinhold Niebuhr, The Irony of American History, Chapter 2: "Innocent Nation Innocent World"

The Cost of Discipleship: Christ Between Us

6th January 2022 at 7:21pm
Dietrich Bonhoeffer The Cost of Discipleship

Below is a quote by Dietrich Bonhoeffer that I point to a lot. It is from his book The Cost of Discipleship

“[Jesus] stands between us and God, and for that very reason he stands between us and all other men and things. He is the Mediator, not only between God and man, but between man and man, between man and reality. Since the whole world was created through him and unto him (John 1:3; 1st Cor. 8:6; Heb. 1:2), he is the sole Mediator in the world...

The call of Jesus teaches us that our relation to the world has been built on an illusion. All the time we thought we had enjoyed a direct relation with men and things. This is what had hindered us from faith and obedience. Now we learn that in the most intimate relationships of life, in our kinship with father and mother, bothers and sisters, in married love, and in our duty to the community, direct relationships are impossible. Since the coming of Christ, his followers have no more immediate realities of their own, not in their family relationships nor in the ties with their nation nor in the relationships formed in the process of living. Between father and son, husband and wife, the individual and the nation, stands Christ the Mediator, whether they are able to recognize him or not. We cannot establish direct contact outside ourselves except through him, through his word, and through our following of him. To think otherwise is to deceive ourselves.

But since we are bound to abhor any deception which hides the truth from our sight, we must of necessity repudiate any direct relationship with the things of this world–and that for the sake of Christ. Wherever a group, be it large or small, prevents us from standing alone before Christ, wherever such a group raises a claim of immediacy it must be hated for the sake of Christ. For every immediacy, whether we realize it or not, means hatred of Christ, and this is especially true where such relationships claim the sanctions of Christian principles.

There is no way from one person to another. However loving and sympathetic we try to be, however sound our psychology, however frank and open our behavior, we cannot penetrate the incognito of the other man, for there are no direct relationships, not even between soul and soul. Christ stands between us, and we can only get into touch with our neighbors through him. That is why intercession is the most promising way to reach our neighbors, and corporate prayer, offered in the name of Christ, the purest form of fellowship.”

Glory

The word "glory" in scripture refers to the essential worth, beauty and value of people, created things and, of course, the Creator Himself. The Hebrew word for glory is a word meaning weight, substance, and at the same time, brilliance or radiant beauty. To glorify someone is to recognize their intrinsic worth and beauty, and to speak of that feature in a public way.

Perspectives: The Story of His Glory pg 49 by Hawthorne

All the nations you have made shall come and worship before you, O LORD, and shall glorify your name.

Psalm 86:9

See also Philippians 3:3.

The idea of "glory" also describes honor that can be given or awarded. When someone is exalted or made great, they are, to some extent, in a biblical sense, glorified. God is so rich in glory that He bestows extravagant honors upon His human servants without compromising His own majesty in the slightest.

Perspectives: The Story of His Glory pg 50 by Hawthorne

John 5:44 shows Jesus pointing out that we glorify one another but yet failed to "seek the glory that is from the one and only God."

The Name of God

Functions of the name of God: reference, revelation, and reputation should be distinct and can be identified via categories: name-tag, window, and fame-name. Concept by Hawthorne pg 50 of Perspectives.

  • Name-Tag Names: Names that God uses to refer to Himself only, a reference
    • Ex: Lord of hosts, God almighty, King of glory, etc.
  • Window: Names that God reveals to us to better understand Him
    • Ex: The Lord is my Shepherd
  • Fame Name: Idea of God's public renown
    • Ex: "God's name" or "the name of God"
    • Isaiah 12:4 - "make known His deeds among the peoples," so that the nations are steadily reminded that "His name is exalted"
    • "It is the open memory, based on historical incidents, which establishes a reputation worthy of future trust." Perspectives: The Story of His Glory pg 50 by Hawthorne.

Abraham

Abraham was not what we would consider as a great evangelist (Genesis 20:11). Yet his heart started in the right place with the establishment of a location to worship God (Genesis 12:7-8).

Proving moment of Abraham's life was a worship event (Genesis 22).
